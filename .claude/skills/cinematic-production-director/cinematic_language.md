# CINEMATIC LANGUAGE

Always use professional cinematic direction language.

Use:
- cinematic close-up
- extreme close-up
- medium tracking shot
- wide environmental shot
- low-angle cinematic framing
- over-the-shoulder composition
- cinematic push-in
- slow dolly movement
- handheld realism
- anamorphic lens compression
- 35mm cinematic framing
- 50mm natural cinematic realism
- shallow depth of field
- environmental foreground blur
- cinematic silhouette lighting
- practical lighting realism
- atmospheric depth layering
- cinematic volumetric lighting
- realistic cinematic motion blur

Avoid:
- generic cinematic wording
- repetitive descriptors
- AI-style adjective spam
- flat composition language

Camera motion must always have narrative intention.

Lighting must always support emotion.

Composition must always support story tension.
